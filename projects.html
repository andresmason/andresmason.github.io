  • Am also considering starting a forex trading dataset through a club at CMU (to operate with CMU funding and use it for a polygon subscription). Thought process:
    • Prop shops can make successful, net positive trades/exchanges near instantly largely due to their datasets that analyze second by second market status. I seek to emulate a small facet of their business, and create a dataset for analyzing forex floating point exchange rates to execute positive currency exchanges. One large barrier of entry to doing this is the frequency I, as an individual, can query the market through Polygon's API or otherwise. Since services like these are so expensive, the clubs function would be to create the architecture for a worker owned base dataset. Once the dataset is laid out, we can pool money for a premium Polygon subscription and use our high call rate to fill our datatable. We would then use Tensorflow and numpy to create a model based off of it. I aim to collect 10 years of historical exchange rate data. Using this base set, individuals in the club will have access to it to use it for their personal algorithmic trading. Ideally, the club would also run the model once a day/week/etc. and then split profits among members, functioning as a coop.
